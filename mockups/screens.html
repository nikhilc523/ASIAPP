When an ASI student government seat becomes vacant mid-year, what is the official appointment process, and who is involved?
ASI
ASI uses a formal Vacant Position Appointment Policy for mid-year vacancies. The policy is written to guide the Executive VP/Chief of Staff and the ASI Personnel Committee through the full procedure for recruiting, interviewing, and appointing students into vacant ASI Student Government positions. [1]
Who is involved (as explicitly named by the policy):
  • Executive VP/Chief of Staff (process support + coordination role described in the policy’s purpose/aim). [1]
  • ASI Personnel Committee (the policy is designed to guide this committee for recruitment/interviews/appointments). [1]
The policy also ties vacancy appointments back to bylaws-based authority and governance documents (use the Admin Manual + Policies index when you need cross-references). [2] [3]
How to use this in real life (what a student actually does):
  • Open the Vacant Position Appointment Policy and identify the section describing recruitment/outreach and interview procedure. [1]
  • Confirm any additional requirements or governance constraints in the Administrative Manual (if your question is “who confirms,” “what committee vote,” “how it’s recorded”). [2]
  • If you’re trying to cite the rule in a memo or meeting: cite the policy PDF directly (it’s the cleanest authority to reference). [1]

The resolution is written as a formal request for campus implementation and defines what the waiver program should look like, including eligibility structure and implementation expectations.

Pioneer Pride Tuesdays
Win Prizes! Win a Scholarship! Go Pioneers!

About the Program

Launched by the ASI Board of Directors in Fall 2023, Pioneer Pride Tuesdays celebrates school spirit and increases student engagement with ASI. During Fall and Spring semesters, students can wear their best red and black, check in at the ASI Office, and enter to win prizes.

Here is how it works

  • Every Tuesday between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM, visit the ASI Office (North UU-314) wearing CSUEB gear/colors. One office visit per week.
  • Show your Bay Card (physical or virtual) to front desk staff.
  • Staff count the items you are wearing. You get one entry per item worn.
  • Provide your name, NetID, and email for your entries that day.
  • Entries are pooled each week, and one winner is drawn on Thursday.
  • Weekly winners are entered into the end-of-semester drawing for a $1,000 scholarship (credited to student account; no cash award).

Acceptable Items

Acceptable items include hats, socks, pants, keychains, buttons, shirts, shorts, sweatshirts, lanyards, and shoes. Buttons or pins count as one entry total, no matter how many are worn.

Other Important Details

  • Weekly Entry: Students are tracked on a spreadsheet by entry count. For drawings, each entry becomes an individual raffle ticket and winners are posted on ASI social media.
  • Weekly Winners: Winners are notified via Horizon email with prize pickup details.
  • Grand Prize Entry: Weekly winners from the current semester are entered into the $1,000 scholarship raffle, drawn on ASI social media around finals week (date TBD).
  • Grand Prize Winner and Scholarship Reporting: Winner must be enrolled the immediate next semester. Scholarship is added to student account and reported to Financial Aid. No cash award is given.
  • Eligibility: Only currently enrolled students can participate. ASI Board of Directors and ASI student staff are not permitted to participate.
  • Program Expectations: Requirements are reviewed annually for program success. Students should consult Financial Aid for aid/cost-of-attendance questions.

Financial Aid Notice

Federal regulations do not allow CSU East Bay to provide financial aid recipients with funding in excess of their Cost of Attendance or Need as calculated by FAFSA/CADAA (Need = COA - EFC). If added funding creates an excess, funds may replace other aid in your package in an order intended to be in your best interest. This may include replacing loans or need-based aid you could otherwise use in subsequent terms. If your aid package exceeds Cost of Attendance or Need after these funds are added, the Office of Financial Aid and Scholarships is required to adjust your aid and will contact you via Horizon email. Aid adjustments may result in an outstanding balance you are responsible for settling. Before spending funds, contact the Office of Financial Aid and Scholarships to confirm whether an adjustment is expected. The California Student Aid Commission also requires additional aid reporting for Middle Class Scholarship recipients, which may reduce MCS and require returning part of issued funds to CSAC. For questions, contact the Office of Financial Aid and Scholarships.
